# Fractions and Ratios

## Learning objectives
- Identify equivalent fractions.
- Explain the relationship between ratios and fractions.
- Apply the rules for comparing and adding fractions.
- Contrast the concepts of ratios and fractions.
- Predict the result of multiplying fractions.

## Key concepts
### Equivalent Fractions
- Equivalent fractions are fractions that represent the same amount.
- They can be found by multiplying or dividing both the numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number.
- The fraction 1/2 is equivalent to 2/4 and 4/8.

### Ratios
- A ratio compares two quantities.
- It can be written as A:B or as A/B.
- The ratio 6:2 simplifies to 3:1.

### Fractions
- A fraction is a part of a whole, represented by the numerator and denominator.
- The numerator indicates the number of parts you have, and the denominator indicates the total number of equal parts in the whole.
- The fraction 3/4 means three out of four equal parts.

## Worked example
- The ratio of apples to oranges in a basket is 6:2, which simplifies to 3:1. If the basket contains 6 apples and 2 oranges, what is the ratio of apples to oranges? The ratio is 6:2, which simplifies to 3:1. This demonstrates the concept of simplifying ratios.

## Common misconceptions
- Adding fractions means adding the numerators and the denominators separately.
- A bigger denominator always means a bigger fraction.
- Ratios, fractions, and percentages are three views of the same idea — the relationship between a part and a whole.
