# Homework — Photosynthesis: How Plants Make Their Own Food

## Practice problems
1. In Mark Felton, a train engineer needs to ensure the train's brakes are functioning correctly. Describe the first two stages of photosynthesis, including where they occur in the plant cell. (maps to: Chloroplasts)
2. In DK Smithsonian "Dinosaurs!", a paleontologist is studying a newly discovered dinosaur fossil. Explain how chlorophyll works to capture sunlight energy. What happens to this captured energy? (maps to: Chlorophyll)
3. In Pacific Northwest narrow-gauge railroads, a track inspector is checking the integrity of the rails. What are the three ingredients a plant needs for photosynthesis to occur? (maps to: Chloroplasts)
4. In Train Simulator Classic, a train driver is managing the train's energy consumption. Describe the role of the light reaction in photosynthesis. What products are formed during this stage? (maps to: The Light Reaction and Calvin Cycle)
5. In PBS NOVA, a biologist is investigating the impact of deforestation on global oxygen levels. Why is photosynthesis important for the Earth's atmosphere? What gas is produced during photosynthesis and why is it essential for animals and humans? (maps to: Chloroplasts)

## Reflection prompt
How might changes in sunlight intensity affect the rate of photosynthesis in a plant?
