# Homework — Photosynthesis: How Plants Make Their Own Food

## Practice problems
1.  A plant is exposed to sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. Describe the first two stages of photosynthesis, including where they occur in the plant cell. (maps to: Chloroplasts)
2.  Explain how chlorophyll works to capture sunlight energy. What happens to this captured energy? (maps to: Chlorophyll)
3.  What are the three ingredients a plant needs for photosynthesis to occur? (maps to: Chloroplasts)
4.  Describe the role of the light reaction in photosynthesis. What products are formed during this stage? (maps to: The Light Reaction and Calvin Cycle)
5.  Why is photosynthesis important for the Earth's atmosphere? What gas is produced during photosynthesis and why is it essential for animals and humans? (maps to: Chloroplasts)

## Reflection prompt
How might changes in sunlight intensity affect the rate of photosynthesis in a plant?
