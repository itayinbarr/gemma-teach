### Teaching basics
- A good lesson plan opens with 3–5 concrete **learning objectives** (verbs like *identify*, *explain*, *apply*).
- **Key concepts** should be small, named, and stand alone; each gets one short paragraph or 2–4 bullets.
- A single **worked example** anchors abstraction in a concrete instance.
- Track 2–4 **common misconceptions** per topic so the teacher can pre-empt them.
- Homework problems should map 1-to-1 onto key concepts and grow in difficulty. Mark the mapping inline: each problem ends with ` (maps to: <Concept Name>)`.
- When tailoring a lesson to a student, **change the framing and examples, not the objectives or problem count**.
- **Operational tailoring**: the worked example and at least one bullet per concept must use a specific student interest (a literal show / game / hobby / activity from their `tags.json`) as the *scenario* — not just as a substituted noun. Operational means the example's reasoning path lives inside the chosen interest; cosmetic means the interest is sprinkled in as a label.
